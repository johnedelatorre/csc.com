Wishing you a Black Death-free holiday season!

2011-12-06

Feature: Advanced Development of rBV A/B and rF1V vaccines

In conjunction with the U.S. Army Medical Research Institute of Infectious Diseases (USAMRIID), DVC has published an update on the status of our botulinum (rBV A/B) and plague (rF1V) vaccine candidates. We also discuss some challenges we've encountered during the development of these vaccines in the journal Advances in Preventive Medicine.

TechWatch: Science News Tidbits

Scientists crack Black Death's genetic code
“Scientists have mapped out the entire genetic map of the Black Death, a 14th century bubonic plague that killed 50 million Europeans in one of the most devastating epidemics in history.” (Source: Reuters; Image from istockphoto, taken at Greyfriars Kirk cemetery, Edinburgh)

Antibody treatment protects monkeys from Hendra virus
A human antibody given to monkeys infected with the deadly Hendra virus completely protected them from disease, according to a study published by National Institutes of Health (NIH) scientists and their collaborators. Hendra and the closely related Nipah virus, both rare viruses that are part of the NIH biodefense research program, target the lungs and brain and have human case fatality rates of 60 percent and more than 75 percent, respectively. (Source: NIH)

Wired petri dish gives real-time updates
“A new prototype petri dish can create an image of what's growing on it and send that information to a laptop, all from inside an incubator. The prototype, dubbed the ePetri, was created from Lego blocks and a cell-phone image sensor, and uses light from a Google Android smart phone.” (Source: MIT Technology Review)

Malaria vaccine trial shows promise
Video (2:06): A malaria vaccine candidate by GSK and PATH appears to cut infections by half in a one-year period. (Source: PharmPro/CNN) In related news, Reuters reports on this Phase 3 trial and the New England Journal of Medicine provides editorial coverage.

A new Johns Hopkins University program offers a Master’s in Biotechnology Enterprise and Entrepreneurship in Rockville or Baltimore, MD or fully online. Contact JHU admissions for more information.

In a scary first, an Ebola-like filovirus was discovered in Europe. (Source: PLoS Pathogens)

The National Science Advisory Board for Biosecurity issued a report in September: Guidance for Enhancing Personnel Reliability and Strengthening the Culture of Responsibility (PDF, 1.2MB)

A genetically engineered smallpox vaccine reduced the risk of death for patients with advanced liver cancer by nearly 60 percent in a mid-stage study. (Source: Reuters)

The Labor Department adjusted a recent regulation requiring contractors to "offer available jobs to employees from the incumbent companies that lost the re-compete of a service contract, sparing those located in highly underutilized business zones." (Source: Washington Business Journal)
