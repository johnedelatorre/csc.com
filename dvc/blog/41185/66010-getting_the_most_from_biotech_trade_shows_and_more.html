Getting the most from biotech trade shows, and more!

2011-06-09

Feature: How to Get More From Your Trade Show Investment

Perhaps unsurprisingly, there’s an app for that (several actually). You might also like this handy checklist of things to do before the show.

If you prefer a more low-tech approach, bring a small stapler and notebook. After you make a new contact, add that person’s card to your notebook and jot some quick notes on what you discussed.  Most importantly, make a note on when/how to follow up, then actually do it.

If you aren’t a natural at networking, try a new approach.  Instead of launching into your elevator speech, ask new contacts what you can do to help them. If you are sincere (that is, actually willing to help), people will remember this, and even if they are not a good candidate for your product or service, they’ll be more likely to recommend you to someone who is if you’ve made a positive impression.

If you’re responsible for your company’s exhibit, Demand Metric offers many useful, free templates for trade show planning and evaluation.

Again, after the show, don’t forget to follow up—this will set you apart from the many who do not. Have biotech trade show tips to share? Post them in the comments section below.

TechWatch: Science News Tidbits

Immunology: How dengue virus hijacks autophagy to fuel its own replication
“Autophagy allows a cell to reuse essential molecules, eliminate bacterial invaders, or undergo programmed cell death. Nicholas Heaton and Glenn Randall at the University of Chicago discovered that dengue virus commandeers the autophagosome to convert cytoplasmic droplets of fatty acids into energy, for efficient viral replication.”  (Source: The Scientist; Image credit:
CDC/Frederick Murphy, Cynthia Goldsmith)

An Inactivated Cell-Culture Vaccine against Yellow Fever
“A two-dose regimen of the XRX-001 vaccine, containing inactivated yellow fever antigen with an alum adjuvant, induced neutralizing antibodies in a high percentage of subjects. XRX-001 has the potential to be a safer alternative to live attenuated 17D vaccine." Yellow fever is a lethal viral hemorrhagic fever. Trial funded by Xcellerex; ClinicalTrials.gov number NCT00995865. (Source: New England Journal of Medicine)

USAMRIID Begins Clinical Trial of New Vaccine to Protect Against Ricin Toxin
A Phase 1 clinical trial to evaluate a new vaccine against ricin toxin is< underway at the U.S. Army Medical Research Institute of Infectious Diseases (USAMRIID). ClinicalTrials.gov number NCT01317667. (Source: USAMRIID press release/Fierce Biotech)

Artificial nose can quickly sniff out bacterial infections
“A researcher at the University of Illinois has created a cheap, disposable 'nose' that can correctly sniff out bacterial infections in only a few hours... Each species of bacteria produces its own unique blend of gases, and even differing strains of the same species will have an aromatic 'fingerprint.' Experienced microbiologists can take a whiff and identify the bacteria.”(Source: Fierce Biotech)
