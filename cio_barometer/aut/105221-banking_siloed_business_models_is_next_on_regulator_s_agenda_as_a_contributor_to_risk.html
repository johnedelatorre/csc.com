Banking: Siloed Business Models is Next on Regulator's Agenda as a Contributor to Risk

Siloed business units have long been a challenge for banks and other financial institutions. Now regulators see disparate operations as a major obstacle to effective risk management and want to see effective mitigation plans put in place. In breaking down silos, financial services firms have a chance to adopt IT solutions that not only effectively address new regulatory scrutiny, but also improve agility and customer service.

By Eric Bass

Ours is an age of unprecedented regulatory activism. It’s a post-crash development that shows no sign of easing any time soon. Financial institutions (FIs) must respond effectively to new regulations, but done right, regulatory compliance can help make FIs more competitive as well.

The Dodd-Frank Act and the constant revisions to existing rules such as the PAT RIOT Act (Anti-Money Laundering, or AML), Know Your Customer (KYC) and Bank Secrecy Act (BSA ) are just a few in a long and growing list of regulations with which FIs must now contend. New regulatory bodies, including the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), are also emerging on the scene. Many firms are responding to the changing regulatory environment by significantly changing the role of IT and the CIO.

CIOs must now get involved, in large part because FIs have historically arranged their business processes and supporting information systems in isolated silos. Imagine a firm that has six separate businesses, each with its own “account open” process and platform. In such a setup, collecting information to satisfy the heightened demands of regulators would be difficult, slow and costly.

Until quite recently, regulators did not fully appreciate the impact of the silo problem. But today that’s changed; regulators get it. While they now appreciate the complexity of siloed businesses, regulators won’t accept the lack of adequate risk management practices that silos produce. While that’s a favorable development for the overall market, it also raises the bar for compliance. FIs are now under greater pressure than ever before from regulators to break down their business process and information silos.

Regulators are requiring FIs to improve their technology infrastructures, bolster security and strengthen privacy protections. And because good risk management is difficult to provide in a siloed business, regulators are asking these firms to improve their horizontal view of their businesses.

Silo wreckers

Cloud-enabled applications, known collectively as Software as a Service (SaaS), are helping FIs break down their silos and begin strategically replacing multiple platforms with a secure, single platform. These are solutions a regulator can love; they help FIs manage risk, but also provide regulators with access to more information about that risk, so regulators can share in the risk-management challenge.

Cloud-based services help FIs create a central repository of data and applications that they can then use for real-time risk management analytics. Cloud computing also helps CIOs shift their costs to a more flexible and variable “pay as you use” model, giving FIs an effective lever to press during times of high volatility.

The importance of the cloud is evident in CSC’s CIO Barometer 2013 report, which is based on a survey of nearly 700 senior IT executives in 18 countries. The survey found that more than 70 percent of respondents mentioned cloud computing as their most important current and emerging challenge; by 2015, managing the cloud will be an even bigger challenge than providing IT and network security. The survey also revealed a significant change in the role of the CIO as well. Most respondents report an increasing involvement in the firm’s core business strategy.

As regulators become more interested with the business process related to identifying and managing risk, new solutions need to be considered and deployed. Siloed data, which used to be primarily a marketing concern, is now on the regulator’s agenda and will therefore be addressed with much greater priority. CIOs can and should play a central role in adopting IT solutions that not only effectively address new regulatory scrutiny, but also improve the organization’s ability to develop greater agility and improved service to its customers. Those FIs that implement technology to channel and manage these market forces will be the ones best positioned for success.

Eric Bass is managing partner, Financial Services, at CSC, a provider of technology-enabled solutions with some 81,000 employees in more than 70 countries.
