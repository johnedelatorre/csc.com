How is the role of the CIO and technology changing in your industry? Read our Viewpoint articles that are written by CSC’s lead consultants and industry specialists. They explain what the CIO Barometer results mean to your industry and what opportunities lie ahead for you.

Automotive: IT Is Now in the Driver's Seat

By Manfred H. Heisen, practice partner, Automotive, for Central and Eastern Europe at CSC
The automotive industry is experiencing another revolution, similar in significance and other ways to the one that occurred a century ago. New, IT-powered developments are transforming the industry to address not only the needs of the new generation of automotive customers — digital natives — but all customers globally.

Banking: Siloed Business Models is Next on Regulator's Agenda as a Contributor to Risk

By Eric Bass, managing partner, Financial Services, at CSC
Siloed business units have long been a challenge for banks and other financial institutions. Now regulators see disparate operations as a major obstacle to effective risk management and want to see effective mitigation plans put in place. In breaking down silos, financial services firms have a chance to adopt IT solutions that not only effectively address new regulatory scrutiny, but also improve agility and customer service.

Capital Markets: Reinventing the CIO in Capital Market

By Loïc Rochas, partner, Global Financial Services Consulting – Capital Markets, at CSC
Chief information offi cers in capital markets are under pressure to cut costs, secure systems and support mobile devices. Yet their greatest opportunities will come from developing systems that add value to the customer relationship.

Chemicals: The New Business Catalyst

By Ed Trevisani, partner and Americas Chemical practice leader at CSC
Today’s chemicals makers urgently need to become more innovative and efficient. With information increasingly an essential component of chemicals products, the industry needs its CIOs to play new, strategic roles.

All Industry: From CIO to CEO

By Dean Nelson, practice director, IT Advisory Services — Americas, at CSC
Could your organization’s next CIO be someone from marketing, sales, manufacturing or even finance? Alternatively, could CIOs with leadership skills strategically position themselves for promotion to a COO, or even CEO, position? Yes — but only for those IT leaders finally able to leave the “speeds and feeds” behind.

Manufacturing: Factory Floor "Secret": We've Gone Mobile

By Stéphane Plovier, vice president of Global Business Consulting at CSC
Factory workers aren’t waiting for company approval to use the latest mobile technology — they’re bringing their own tablets and smartphones onto the factory fl oor. It’s a trend that smart CIOs will embrace, not fi ght.

Automotive: CIO = Collaborative Information Officer

By Douglas Maulbetsch, managing partner, global automotive, at CSC
Thanks to several new technologies, the auto industry now has the tools to transform the customer’s buying experience. But to harness those tools, leaders of automotive IT, marketing and sales will need to learn how to work together.

Utilities: Why Utilities CIOs Need to Lead

By Jeffrey Provine, global practice leader for the CSC Utilities Consulting practice
The utilities industry is being transformed on multiple fronts, from the regulatory environment to social media, where new channels are empowering customers — and all of these fronts are heavily dependent on IT. CIOs and other IT leaders need to play a more strategic role in defi ning a utility company’s new relationship with customers.
