Technology Trends & Future Predictions 2013

CSC’s technology trends and future predictions for 2013 looks back over 2012 and assesses, in four short papers, the key technologies and trends and issues likely to be top of mind for financial services in 2013.

What does 2013 hold in store? Technology is evolving rapidly and many believe that the IT trends identified for 2012 in financial services – and wider industry - will continue to remain top of the agenda for 2013.

Technology trends and future predictions for 2013 asserts that the combined forces associated with social, mobile, cloud and information – innovative and disruptive on their own – together are revolutionising business and society, disrupting old business models and creating new leaders.

IT Trends for 2013 - A Perspective

In what will likely be another tough year for financial services businesses – in Australia and globally – IT budgets will be pared and things that were not deferred in 2012, probably will be reviewed again in 2013 for deferral. No surprises that many financial services organisations, already asset-sweating very tired IT assets to keep a lid on costs while revenue growth is minimal, will continue to sweat them and the now familiar ‘pretty pigs’ will be re-lipsticked!

Deep marketing budgets will enable institutions to paint a typically rosy picture to the consumer-punter, but only the regulatory imposed agenda investments will have priority.

Nevertheless, as the momentum behind technology-enabled business growth and innovation remains strong and IT investment does not come to a complete stop in 2013 as in-flight projects continue.

Cloud and "as-a-service" will continue to be considered as a method of consumption; value creation from Enterprise IT and modernisation of IT; analytics and managing 'big data'; end-user virtualisation and security, amongst others, will all remain high on the agenda. These elements are covered in the first essay ‘IT trends for 2013 – a perspective’.


The Connected Consumer

But more than that, four key themes (mobile, micro, media and mining) will underpin a shift in the way people – Consumers – use technology, posing a challenge to financial services providers. ‘The Connected Consumer’ essay deals with this.


The War for your Digital Wallet

The revolution in payments using mobile devices is nothing short of extraordinary – and consumers will choose winners. And better, stronger, faster banking technology will increasingly differentiate ‘trust’ in banking … trust in financial services as an industry. Two essays, ‘The war for your digital wallet’ and ‘Ready Tech Go’ cover these topics and thematic.
