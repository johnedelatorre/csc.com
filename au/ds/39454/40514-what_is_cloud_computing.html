    • What is Cloud Computing?

    Flash

    Cloud computing is a technology innovation that enables computing power and services to be delivered and accessed through the Internet, provided as a service instead of as a product. Customers access applications, operating platforms, data storage and other computing resources via the web and do not need knowledge of or expertise in managing the underlying technology infrastructure.

    Virtualisation means a business can infinitely scale its technology to meet demand in real time. Any organisation can gain incredible cost savings from no longer having to purchase, maintain and upgrade expensive hardware or software licenses. Anyone on the team will have the potential to create new business value through collaboration and the reinvention of business processes and relationships.

    The cloud offers any organisation the following benefits:

    ::. Customers generally do not own the infrastructure, they merely access or consume resources as a service
    . No upfront capital costs are required
    . There are no increased investments for peak loads â€” users simply scale up the resources they need
    . Users receive the same service from anywhere around the world and from any internet-connected device

    The cloud isn't a one-size-fits-all solution, however. Businesses have unique requirements, and some will be service-driven while others will focus on the storage needs. Therefore, cloud users will require various levels of security, service level agreements and governance.

    As cloud computing becomes part of an integrated solution, there will be many issues and concerns that will need to be addressed. These include control issues, such as security, privacy and lock-in; and service-level issues, such as integration, reliability and availability. Additionally, it will be important that organisations look at software licensing and governance in the cloud.
