Quintessential Guide to Buying Cloud

One credit card and a few clicks to provision a 2x4 – pricing can be that simple. On the other hand, doing real work on the cloud requires more than simple capacity: There are concerns over security and governance for compliance, access controls and ensuring an “always on” state for the cloud. It’s important to understand the impact these requirements will have on the price of cloud.

Inadequate support by cloud providers, a lengthy list of add-on features, unclear service level agreements and unpredictable billing affect the total cost of ownership (TCO). How much do benefits like innovation, agility and consistency really cost on the cloud?

For CIOs and sourcing professionals, we’re offering a guide to buying cloud written by the Outsourcing Center. It covers all the things that can add cost to a simple rate card and provides guidance for moving from a traditional outsourcer to a cloud provider.
