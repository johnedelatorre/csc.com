Success Stories

The Brussels Regional Informatics Centre (BRIC) tests its mobile applications in the cloud

 

Client:

Brussels Regional Informatics Centre (BRIC)

Challenge:

  • The Brussels-Capital Region sought a more efficient way to help employees and citizens report infrastructure maintenance needs

Solution:

  • Design of the convenient, rapid-alert "FixMyStreet" app, leveraging a reusable, cloud-based multidevice mobile app platform

Results:

  • Development time reduced by 65%
  • Development costs reduced by 75%
  • Contact us
    Contact us

Integrating mobility into the strategy

As a Public Interest Organisation, the BRIC is the neutral technology partner of the public institutions in Brussels that proactively make use of IT to optimise the efficiency and customer-friendliness of the services they offer to citizens, businesses and visitors to the Brussels-Capital Region. One year ago, the BRIC wanted to integrate mobility into its strategy. Three projects contributed to the development of this strategy: “FixMyStreet”, initiated in the framework of the strategic IT plan of the Brussels Minister in charge of IT, Brigitte Grouwels, to enable inhabitants to report defects and problems encountered in public spaces (roads, sidewalks, traffic lights, etc.) via a central digital information point. It will be possible to submit this information via the website, by SMS or using a smartphone. This application will go live at the beginning of 2013. In parallel, two other projects contributed to the increasing momentum of the BRIC’s mobility strategy: the roll-out of a mobile version of the web portal of the Brussels Region, as well as the development of mobile applications for Brussels Mobility, the administration in charge of equipment and infrastructure related to moving around in the Brussels Region. BRIC didn’t want to undertake the implementation of its mobility strategy alone and thus called upon CSC in Belgium for guidance. “We didn’t have any internal competence in the area of mobility, and BRIC didn’t want to set out on its own. We were looking for a technical expert. CSC submitted a well-structured mobile proposal, which guaranteed us the support we wanted (a network of developers, methodology and knowledge of the sector), and that’s what convinced us to work with them,” explains Éric Auquière, Head of Information Systems at BRIC.

An automated development centre hosted in the cloud

After having developed a first hybrid application, BRIC fundamentally re-assessed its mobility strategy and decided to launch a new service focusing on the development of automated mobile cross-platform applications, MSTC: Mobile Software Technical Centre. This infrastructure enables the development of mobile applications of all kinds: native, hybrid and web, thanks to a large number of reusable reference mobile solutions delivered by CSC. One of the innovative aspects was in the testing phase. “Given that we’ve got to cope with a large variety of mobile devices, CSC proposed that we use a cloud-based testing service (provided by the company Perfecto Mobile) which tests the applications on various models of mobile devices — using both iOS and Android. CSC also created within MSTC a specific integration layer which enables fine interaction with the cloud-based system as well as the automation of this process within the production chain while ensuring the optimisation of cloud time. This layer makes it possible to continuously test applications on dozens of mobiles at low cost, after every modification in the code. As a public service, we could never have approved a similar level of investment in testing equipment. Simply buying machine time was in fact the best solution,” adds Éric Auquière.

Open data and knowledge transfer

As a public service, BRIC wanted to promote open data. Éric Auquière explains: “By taking an open data approach, we’re making the data accessible to the largest number of people and making it possible for third parties to develop applications that draw on that data.” Furthermore, in line with a philosophy of making optimum use of public finances, BRIC intends over the long term to ensure the transfer of knowledge and best practices — an approach suggested by CSC at the begin of the cooperation between the two organisations.

First mobile applications on line

“We’re very happy with the collaboration with CSC in the framework of the development of our mobile solutions. In a six-month time frame, two applications have already been developed and other projects are underway. The implementation by CSC of the Mobile Software Technical Centre facilitates and accelerates the production process and significantly reduces the cost of developing and maintaining the mobile applications of the Brussels Regional Informatics Centre,” concludes Éric Auquière.
